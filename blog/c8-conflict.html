Conflict Research | Nov 9, 2014

Sibling conflicts are always fun. I have an older brother and a younger sister. When we get into arguments it is funny to see each other's personality shine. An example of this is with my older brother. I remember it was over our cell phone contract. I wanted to move over to T-mobile to save some money. However I ended our then current plan with AT&T prematurely before our new contract with T-mobile was setup. So we were out of cell phone service for one week. I know this is first world problems but my brother was pissed about how I could be so careless.

His compass of shame is to attack other. He would say how stupid and careless I was. He wanted me to call either service right away so that he could have service asap! Well, in a conflict I choose the withdrawal method. I am usually quiet and chill during this situations. This case was also similar. I was quiet and didn't respond to him but in the background I did try to solve the issue but calling T-mobile to expedite the sim cards and if we could visit a local store to activate in 3 days instead of the original week.

My actions made the conflict better in my opinion. To me it is not good when two people are yelling and trading insults and past, failed examples during a conflict. I'm not saying I'm not guilty of this but in these conflicts I try and keep an even keel. I think this helped calm the tense situation and everything was better. I remember reading a quote somewhere that a husband and wife should never yell or argue with each other unless the house was on fire! I try to keep with this quote whenever a conflict arises. For future conflicts, I think it is better if I explain what and how I am trying to solve the problem during the conflict instead of waiting till it's over. One of my personality traits is to do/fix things quietly. In the future I hope to diffuse the situation faster by saying how I will fix the problem and what the lessons I learned were.
