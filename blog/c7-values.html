I did it! | Nov 2, 2014

It may be cliche but accomplishments is the value that has made me the most proudest and satisfied in my life. Whether it was a longer term thing like studying and working hard to graduate from college. Or spending a couple of hours to break down a coding challenge and finally having everything "click" in your mind are great feelings. Seeing the Bingo Board displayed with the correct "x's" marked was a great accomplishment for me. Accomplishments at work makes me feel good such as when the Director of Engineering says that I will be greatly missed when I leave for DBC. It means that I connected well with my co-workers and garnered great domain knowledge at my job. Like most everyone, I want to feel appreciated and respected for what I have done. When you accomplish something that was tough for you in the beginning, it sort of validates and stamps all the hard work that was put into it.

In general I try to live up to the following values on a scale of 1 through 5, with the higher numbers I agree to:
Accountability: 5
Accomplishment: 4
Challenge: 2
Confidence: 4
Self-respect: 5
Wealth: 3
I know that one of my flaws is that I do not push myself as hard as I can and love having a set routine. A good example of this is weightlifting with my cousin. I will suggest doing the same weights and reps over again, while he will constantly suggest moving up.

The last topic someone asked for my advice happened at work. I had to explain to her about how the different features at our company function and how they are integrated together. A co-worker asked me about what an Internal Report did and I gave a detailed explanation for it. Again it feels good because it means that I know and understand the product well enough to explain to a fellow co-worker. I know that DBC will be an intense environment and having the courage and belief to quit you job and change skill sets is an achievement in its own right. I think it takes some guts and decisiveness to pivot to a Developer Role and it makes me feel good that I did it. I already broke my mold of routines and being happy with the status quo. When there is something tough in DBC I need to remember that what we are doing is a pretty darn good achievement in itself. There is a fine line tough, it doesn't mean that I shouldn't study harder to absorb all the tech info. It means being self-aware that choosing to attend DBC is changing your life for the better by increasing your knowledge and varying your learning perspective.
